# project-deep-dive/

## Purpose

Detailed technical narratives for past projects, ready for staff-level "tell me about a project you're proud of" deep dives. Interviewers will drill into architecture, tradeoffs, failures, and your specific contributions — have every layer prepared.

## Planned Contents

One file per project: `<project-name>.md`

## Conventions

Each project file should cover:
1. **Context** — What problem were you solving? Why did it matter?
2. **Your Role** — What were you personally responsible for? Who else was involved?
3. **Architecture** — High-level design, key components, data flow
4. **Key Decisions** — What tradeoffs did you make and why?
5. **Challenges** — What went wrong or was harder than expected?
6. **Outcome** — Measurable results, adoption, business impact
7. **What You'd Do Differently** — Honest reflection

## Tips

- Practice narrating each project in 3–5 minutes before drilling into details.
- Prepare answers for at least 3 levels of "why" on every key decision.
- Know the exact numbers: latency improvements, scale, team size, timeline.

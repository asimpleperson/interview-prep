# Why Anthropic

AI is going to change how people live and work as much as the internet did, and probably faster. How we write code, learn, do research, run companies, make everyday calls — it's already shifting. I want to spend this decade helping build that, not watching from the side.

Here's the honest reason it's Anthropic and not somewhere else. I think a lot of companies can build powerful AI. I think only a few are actually trying to build it in a way that's good for people, and of those few, Anthropic is the one I'd bet on to get it right at scale. That belief is the whole thing for me. The technology is going to be enormous either way. The question that keeps me up is who's steering it, and whether the people steering it really mean it when they say the goal is to leave the world better. With Anthropic I believe they mean it.

The products are how I came to trust that. Claude is what I reach for when I want to think through a hard problem carefully. Claude Code has changed how I write software day to day — it feels like a real collaborator, and it raises what one engineer can get done in a week. A lot of the engineers and researchers I respect have quietly made these tools the center of how they work. You don't get there by cutting corners. You get there by caring about the craft and shipping things that hold up under real use, and that tells me the same care is going into the harder, less visible decisions too.

I also like that Anthropic doesn't treat safety and capability as a trade-off. The bet is that you can lead on both at the same time, and that's exactly how I think about it. I'd much rather help prove that bet right than work somewhere that treats safety as something for the press release.

And at a staff level, this is where my work would actually compound. Building inference infrastructure, agent systems, or the product surface here means the impact lands on how hundreds of millions of people eventually meet AI for the first time. The problems are genuinely hard, the people are people I'd want to learn from, and the mission is one I'd happily defend to a friend over coffee.

I've used Claude almost every day for a long time now, and the feeling I keep coming back to is simple: this is the version of AI I want to exist in the world. I'd like to help build it.

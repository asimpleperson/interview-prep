# Why OpenAI

AI is going to reshape how people live and work on a scale comparable to the internet, and probably faster. I want to spend this decade helping build that, and OpenAI is one of the very few places where the work actually moves that frontier.

The honest reason I care about this company is that ChatGPT changed the world I live in. I remember the first week it came out, and I remember how strange it felt that something this useful had just appeared. A few years later it's the default tool for hundreds of millions of people, and most of them barely think about it anymore. That kind of shift, from "demo" to "infrastructure people forget they rely on," almost never happens. OpenAI made it happen.

It also changed how I personally do research, learn, and write software. Outside of work the loop starts with ChatGPT instead of ten Google tabs. I describe what I'm actually trying to understand, get a real answer back, push on the parts that seem shaky, and follow citations only where it matters. At work it's more Codex. When I'm navigating an unfamiliar codebase, drafting a change across files I don't fully know yet, or trying to get a prototype standing up quickly, Codex is in the loop the whole time. It compresses hours of work into minutes and raises the ceiling on what I can take on alone. I notice it most when I'm forced to work without these tools for a while and everything feels slower and smaller.

That's the product I want to help build. The technical problems at OpenAI are some of the hardest in the industry, from training and inference to product surfaces used by a meaningful fraction of the internet. At a staff-engineer level, the leverage is real. Code I help ship will end up in the loop of how a lot of people learn, work, and think.

I'd like to be one of the people building the next version of that, instead of just using it.
